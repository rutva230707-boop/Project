What is a panic attack?

A panic attack is a sudden rush of intense fear or discomfort that usually peaks within minutes and can include physical symptoms like racing heart and shortness of breath.

Panic attacks can feel scary, but they are not usually dangerous. They often pass even if they feel very strong.

Common signs

  • Racing or pounding heart, chest discomfort
  • Shortness of breath, feeling like you cannot get enough air
  • Dizziness, shaking, sweating, or feeling very hot or very cold
  • Feeling unreal or detached from your surroundings
  • Fear of losing control, “going crazy,” or dying

Grounding and calming ideas

  • Slow breathing: Breathe in slowly, hold briefly, and breathe out longer than you breathed in.
  • 5‑4‑3‑2‑1 senses grounding: Notice things you can see, touch, hear, smell, and taste.
  • Cold water or ice: Gently hold something cool or splash your face with cool water.
  • Movement: Walk slowly, stretch, or gently tense and relax muscles.

Questions for during a panic moment

1. Notice your breath

Right now, can you place a hand on your chest or belly and notice your breathing for a few seconds?



2. Try one slow breath

Can you try one slow breath in through your nose and a longer breath out through your mouth?



3. Use your senses

Can you look around and name at least 3 things you can see right now?



4. Use cold or touch

Is there something you can safely hold or touch (like a piece of fabric, chair, water, or ice) to help ground you?



5. Check your safety

Are you in physical danger right now, or are you in a safe place while your body feels afraid?



When to reach out for help

If panic or strong fear is happening often, making you avoid many places, or feels out of control, a mental health professional can help with treatment and coping skills. You can talk to your doctor, a counsellor, or a trusted person in your life about how you are feeling.

If you ever think your symptoms might be from a medical problem, or you feel like you might collapse or be in real physical danger, seek urgent medical help or emergency services.
